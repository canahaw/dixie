About Dixies Salazar  

I have been an artist since I was three and I’ve always made art. Art is my refuge, my silent mate, the repository of the life that has shaped me. I access the subconscious and hidden interior world of the psyche where leaps of perception are juxtaposed, releasing a synergy of metaphor and meaning beyond the images created.

I’ve explored female Anglo and Latina identity and heritage and blend everyday objects and popular culture icons creating a mythos. Underlying dialogues mix with elements of religion, iconoclastic and questioning. My art is my personal self and resonates with universal concern.

Fascinated with Mayan and Aztec cultures and their connection to nature, I studied Mesoamerica. I became aware of a sense of diaspora within myself, and recognized the crisis of contemporary life, all of us caught together in a decaying world far from our homelands.

Dixie Salazar has worked as an artist and writer for more than 40 years. Born in Chicago Dixie is now based in California’s Central Valley where she draws inspiration from the rich history of Spanish and Mexican heritage.
